16th Quadrennial General Conference (QGC)

The Association of African Universities (AAU) is proud to present the 16th Quadrennial General Conference, focusing on shaping the future of higher education for innovation and sustainable development in Africa.

This conference aims to address critical challenges in African higher education, promote innovative approaches, and foster collaboration among institutions across the continent.

Conference Schedule

16th Quadrennial General Conference (QGC) - July 7-11, 2025

Day - 01 Day - 02 Day - 03 Day - 04 Day - 05

9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Opening Conference

Welcome address and introduction to the conference theme: "Shaping the Future of Higher Education for Innovation and Sustainable Development in Africa"

11:00 AM - 12:30 PM

Keynote Speech

Keynote address on the current challenges and opportunities in African higher education

2:00 PM - 4:00 PM

Panel Discussion

Panel discussion on "Policy and Advocacy for Transforming Higher Education

9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

AI and Technology-driven Teaching and Learning

Exploring the integration of AI and advanced technologies in higher education, including adaptive learning platforms, AI-powered smart classrooms, and virtual reality applications in teaching.

11:00 AM - 12:30 PM

Curricula Re-engineering and Credit Transfer Systems

Discussion on re-engineering curricula to be more adaptive and integrative, and establishing seamless credit transfer systems to foster student mobility and lifelong learning.

2:00 PM - 4:00 PM

Collaborative Research and Capacity Building

Presentation of initiatives promoting collaborative research and capacity-building in African higher education, such as the Carnegie African Diaspora Fellowship Program and the Regional Universities Forum for Capacity Building in Agriculture (RUFORUM).

9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Climate Change, Green Energy and Blue Economy

Exploring the role of higher education institutions in addressing climate change, promoting green energy solutions, and developing the blue economy. Case studies from institutions like the University of Cape Town's African Climate and Development Initiative and Nelson Mandela University's Ocean Sciences Campus.

11:00 AM - 12:30 PM

Entrepreneurship Skills and Competencies

Discussion on integrating entrepreneurship education across disciplines, featuring successful models like the African Leadership University and Strathmore University's @iLabAfrica. Focus on developing skills for innovation and sustainable enterprise creation.

2:00 PM - 4:00 PM

Funding Infrastructural Development in Higher Educational Institutions

Exploring innovative funding models for infrastructure development in African higher education, including government initiatives, public-private partnerships, and international collaborations. Case studies on successful funding projects supported by the World Bank and African Development Bank.

9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Indigenous Knowledge and Environmental Preservation

Examining the integration of indigenous knowledge into higher education curricula and its role in environmental conservation. Featuring initiatives like the University of Botswana's Okavango Research Institute and Mbarara University of Science and Technology's Indigenous Knowledge Centre.

11:00 AM - 12:30 PM

Higher Education's Contribution to Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

Exploring how higher education institutions can align their research, teaching, and community engagement with the SDGs. Highlighting initiatives like KNUST's Centre for Sustainable Development and the University of Nairobi's Institute for Climate Change and Adaptation.

2:00 PM - 4:00 PM

Panel Discussion: Future of African Higher Education

A panel discussion bringing together experts to discuss the future of higher education in Africa, focusing on innovation, sustainability, and the role of universities in addressing continental challenges.

9:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Synthesis of Conference Themes

A comprehensive overview of the key insights, innovations, and strategies discussed throughout the conference, highlighting the interconnections between different sub-themes and their collective impact on shaping the future of higher education in Africa.

11:00 AM - 12:30 PM

Action Planning Workshop

Interactive workshop sessions where participants collaborate to develop concrete action plans for implementing the ideas and strategies discussed during the conference. Focus on enhancing access and equity, fostering research and innovation ecosystems, and integrating SDGs into curricula and institutional practices.

2:00 PM - 3:30 PM

Networking and Collaboration Forum

Facilitated networking session to foster collaborations between institutions, researchers, policymakers, and industry partners. Opportunity to form working groups, plan joint research projects, and establish partnerships for implementing innovative educational practices.

4:00 PM - 5:00 PM

Closing Ceremony and Way Forward

Closing remarks summarizing the key outcomes of the conference, presentation of the conference declaration, and outlining the next steps for the Association of African Universities in advancing the transformation of higher education for innovation and sustainable development in Africa.
